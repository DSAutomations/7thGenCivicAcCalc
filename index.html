How to run the performance test

The manual specifies exact test conditions. Deviating from them will shift your readings relative to these charts. [p. 21-98]

  • Avoid direct sunlight.
  • Open the hood and both front doors.
  • Set temperature control to MAX COOL, mode to VENT, recirculation to RECIRCULATE.
  • A/C switch on, fan switch on MAX.
  • Engine at 1,500 rpm. No driver or passengers in the vehicle.
  • Run the A/C for 10 minutes before reading gauges and thermometer.

Where to measure: Insert a thermometer in the centre air vent for delivery temperature. Intake (ambient) temperature is read near the blower unit behind the glove box — this is not the same as outside air temperature and is the value to enter above. [p. 21-98]

Reading the chart: Draw a vertical line up from your intake temperature to your humidity curve. Mark points 10% above and 10% below that humidity level and draw horizontal lines from each across to the delivery temperature axis. Your reading should fall between those two lines. Apply the same method to the pressure charts. Any reading outside the band warrants further inspection. [p. 21-99]

Refrigerant quick reference

  • Type: HFC-134a (R-134a). Use only R-134a gauge sets and equipment — do not substitute.
  • Charge capacity: 500–550 g (17.6–19.4 oz). Overcharging will damage the compressor. [p. 21-96]
  • Charge through the low-pressure side only. Never open the high-pressure valve while charging with refrigerant cans. [p. 21-96]
  • Do not turn refrigerant cans upside down while charging. [p. 21-96]
  • Engine must be running below 1,500 rpm during charging, with A/C on, MAX COOL, fan on MAX, front doors open. [p. 21-96]
  • After charging, check for leaks around the compressor, condenser, and receiver/dryer. [p. 21-96]
  • Never use compressed air to pressure-test or leak-test the system — R-134a mixed with compressed air is combustible. [p. 21-97]

Evacuation

Required any time the system is opened — after repair, component replacement, or accidental discharge. [p. 21-95]

  • If the system was open for several days, replace the receiver/dryer before evacuating. [p. 21-95]
  • Use a vacuum pump adapter with a check valve to prevent pump oil backflow into the system. [p. 21-95]
  • Run the pump for at least 15 minutes. The low-side gauge must reach above 93.3 kPa (700 mmHg / 27.6 in Hg) and hold steady with valves closed. [p. 21-95]
  • If that vacuum is not reached within 15 minutes, there is a leak — find and repair it before continuing. [p. 21-95]
  • If no leaks, continue pumping for at least another 15 minutes, then close valves and stop the pump. [p. 21-95]
  • When discharging before evacuation, let the high-side drop below 140 psi (980 kPa) before opening the low-side valve. Open valves only gradually — discharging too fast draws compressor oil out of the system. [p. 21-94]
